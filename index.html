The Pilgrims Route

A thousand-year-old path that has transformed millions of lives. We start in Madrid, travel by high-speed train to Leon, and ride 340 km through mountains, valleys, and medieval villages until we reach the majestic Obradoiro Square.

Credential and Compostela

Included in your welcome kit. We stamp your pilgrim credential on every stage, and the Compostela - the official pilgrim certificate - is awarded during our farewell gala dinner.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

D1

Madrid | Arrival and Welcome

Check-in at Hotel Chamartin The One or similar. Welcome dinner and briefing to answer questions and prepare for the adventure.

-
D2

Madrid to Leon (High-Speed Train)

Fast train transfer. Personalized bike fitting. Free afternoon to explore Leon. Typical local dinner at Restaurante Ezequiel on Calle Ancha.

-
D3

Leon to Astorga | Stage 1

First riding day through mostly flat terrain before the mountain sections. We cross the historic Roman bridge over the Orbigo River and visit Gaudi's Episcopal Palace in Astorga.

55 km
D4

Astorga to Ponferrada | Stage 2

Climb to Cruz de Ferro pass (1,500 m). A moving ancestral ritual at the summit, then a long descent to the Templar Castle area in Ponferrada.

60 km
D5

Ponferrada to O Cebreiro | Queen Stage

Ride along the Valcarce valley through vineyards, cherry trees, and chestnut forests. Enter Galicia and finish in the pre-Romanesque village of O Cebreiro.

65 km
D6

O Cebreiro to Portomarin | Stage 4

Scenic descent with epic views. Visit the Monastery of Samos and pass the symbolic Km 100 marker before arriving in pilgrim-friendly Portomarin.

65 km
D7

Portomarin to Melide | Stage 5

Enchanting forest corridors and streamside paths. Arrive in Melide, famous for Galician octopus, perfect before the final push to Santiago.

45 km
D8

Melide to Santiago | Final Stage

Lavacolla ascent, Monte do Gozo, and group arrival at Obradoiro Square. Gala dinner and Compostela award ceremony in an unforgettable final night.

55 km
D9

Santiago de Compostela | Free Day

Breakfast and end of official services. Enjoy Santiago at your own pace: cathedral, local markets, and museums. Book your return train in advance.
